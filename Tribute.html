Tribute to Maharana Pratap and Samrat Prithviraj Chauhan

Maharana Pratap

Maharana Pratap Singh, popularly known as Maharana Pratap, was a Rajput King from Mewar, Rajasthan, India.
He is widely regarded as a Brave Warrior who fought against the Mughal emperor Akbar in the 16th century. Maharana Pratap's heroic resistance against the Mughal forces in the Battle of Haldighati is a legendary tale that showcases his valor and dedication to protect his kingdom and people.

Samrat Prithviraj Chauhan

Prithviraj Chauhan was a Rajput King of the Chauhan dynasty who ruled over twin capitals of Delhi and Ajmer region in the 12th century.
He is remembered for his military prowess, chivalry, and his rivalry with the Afghan ruler Muhammad Ghori. Prithviraj Chauhan's famous battles against Muhammad Ghori, especially the Second Battle of Tarain, have become part of Indian folklore, symbolizing his bravery and sacrifice for his land.
